Gardner Center at #AERA15

Gardner Center staff will present on a range of topics at the 2015 annual meeting of the American Educational Research Association (AERA). At a symposium on April 18, a group of Gardner Center researchers will share insights from four case examples of university-community research partnerships. In addition, staff will participate in sessions on family engagement, community development and collaboration, health in education, and more. On Friday, April 17, our Founding Director Milbrey McLaughlin will receive the Division L Lifetime Achievement Award in recognition of her contributions to educational policy research.



GARDNER CENTER SYMPOSIUM

SATURDAY, APRIL 18
Enhancing Knowledge About Data Use and Inquiry in University-Community Research Partnerships: Insights from Four Case Examples
Sheraton, Second Level, Colorado
8:15 a.m. to 9:45 a.m.

Through four in-depth case illustrations of university-community collaborations, we seek to spark a robust discussion about the merits and challenges of research-practice partnerships as a strategy to address enduring problems of practice in education. The papers in this symposium deal with many issues, which range from power dynamics to data use practices to ensuring that partnership processes are respectful of cultural differences. Through these four case examples, we seek to advance knowledge about research-practice partnerships and draw attention to key considerations and practices that facilitate and sustain these types of collaborations.
