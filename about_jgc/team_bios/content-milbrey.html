Milbrey W. McLaughlin

Founding Director
David Jacks Professor of Education and Public Policy, Stanford University

Milbrey McLaughlin is the Founding Director of the John W. Gardner Center for Youth and Their Communities at Stanford University. She is also the Co-Director of the Center for Research on the Context of Teaching, an interdisciplinary research center engaged in analyses of how teaching and learning are shaped by teachers’ organizational, institutional, and social-cultural contexts. As the David Jacks Professor of Education and Public Policy at Stanford University’s School of Education, Dr. McLaughlin’s research combines studies of K-12 education policy in the U.S and work on the broad question of community-school collaboration to support youth development.


At Stanford, Dr. McLaughlin teaches Urban Youth and Their Institutions; Best Practices in Youth Development; Policy Analysis in Education; and Macro and Micro Issues in Policy Analysis. She has published articles and books that consider issues such as youth and community development, policy implementation and situated practice, along with the role of neighborhood organizations in the lives of inner-city youth. Dr. McLaughlin has served as a member of multiple boards, including the National Network for Youth, Children and Youth Community Health Initiative of the California Wellness Foundation, the Irvine Foundation Urban Initiative, Partners in School Innovation and the Center for Research on Equity and Diversity in Education. She was a member of the National Academy of Sciences committee that produced the volume Community Programs to Promote Youth Development and currently serves on the National Academy of Science’s Committee on Adolescent Health and Development.
