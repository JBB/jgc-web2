Brian Holzman

Graduate Research Assistant

Brian Holzman is a doctoral candidate in Sociology & Higher Education at Stanford University. He received a B.S. from the University of Wisconsin-Madison in Sociology, Latin American, Caribbean, and Iberian Studies (LACIS), and Spanish in 2006. Following graduation, Brian interned on the Policy and Budget Team at the Wisconsin Department of Public Instruction and worked as a Senior Program Support Representative at Reading is Fundamental, Inc. He also was a Research Associate at the American Institutes for Research, where he worked on a variety of evaluations and technical assistance centers related to early college high school, high school accountability, and special education. In his doctoral work, Brian uses sociological theory and quantitative methods to examine racial and social inequality in educational performance and choice as well as the role of cultural and social capital in college-going.
