Li Sha

Graduate Research Assistant

Li Sha is a second year doctoral student in Organizational Studies program at the Stanford School of Education. Before she came to Stanford, she completed an undergraduate Honors degree in Biomedical Science at the National University of Singapore. Her past research projects include analyzing the cost-effectiveness of warfarin therapy with the Singapore General Hospital and a biometric study of peat forests in Southeast Asia under the Singapore-MIT Alliance for Research and Technology.


Her current research interests are institutional change in the midst of education or healthcare reform and understanding the effect of cultural differences on business-society collaboration. She spent the past summer in her hometown ChengDu working with the city Reform and Development Commission, where she interviewed public hospitals at township, county and city levels in response to the new health care reform policy. She just received funding from the Gates-CRIS project to study the implementation of College Readiness Indicators in five US school districts.
