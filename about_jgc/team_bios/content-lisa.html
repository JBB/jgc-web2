Lisa Westrich

Research & Policy Analyst

Lisa Westrich joined the staff of the John W. Gardner Center for Youth and Their Communities in November 2009. Prior to that, Lisa worked for three years as a Research and Evaluation Consultant providing services to educational organizations and other nonprofits on issues related to school reform, program implementation, and organizational development. Before working as a consultant, Lisa was a member of the Adolescent Heath Van team at Lucile Packard Children's Hospital at Stanford University, where she provided advocacy and mental health services to homeless and uninsured teens.


Lisa has also worked in nonprofit leadership positions as the Associate Director of Rebuilding Together San Francisco and as the Clinical Director at Legal Services for Children in San Francisco. She has experience in school and clinic-based research, program development and implementation, and has provided clinical consultation to a number of local non-profits. Lisa is a licensed clinical social worker (LCSW) who has worked with children and families in a variety of education and nonprofit settings, with an emphasis on adolescent development, for the last twenty years. Lisa received her MSW from Columbia University and her Bachelor's Degree from U.C. Berkeley.
