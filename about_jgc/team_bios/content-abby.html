Abby Do

Research Assistant

Abby Do joined the Gardner Center in 2014 as a Research Assistant. She is involved with the California Office to Reform Education (CORE) and College Access Foundation of California (CAFC) projects. Her role includes coordinating logistics and communication, researching and drafting materials used in stakeholder involvement, and data cleaning. Previously, she served as a Peace Corps Volunteer in the Republic of Georgia, where she taught English and worked on various local, national, and international youth development projects.


Abby earned an MA in International Comparative Education at the Stanford Graduate School of Education. Her thesis, which examined the tensions between international norms and national legacies of conflict, was a qualitative document analysis of policies surrounding minority medium of instruction schools in Israel and Estonia. She also holds a BA in English, with honors, from Dartmouth College.
