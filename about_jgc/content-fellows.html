Fellows at the Gardner Center


“The world is an incomparable classroom, and life is a memorable teacher for those who aren't afraid of her.” – John W. Gardner

The Gardner Center is proud to foster opportunities for youth and young adults to participate in the fields of youth development and community-based research. This year we are partnering with the Shinnyo-En Foundation, the Haas Center for Public Service, and Public Allies to host two post-undergraduate fellows, Sophia Colombari Figueroa and Cody Young, to collaborate with youth-serving organizations in our near neighbor communities of East Palo Alto and Redwood City, respectively.

2014-15 FELLOWS

Sophia Colombari Figueroa, Stanford Shinnyo Fellow

During her year as a Shinnyo Fellow, Sophia Colombari Figueroa will work with Redwood City 2020 and the John W. Gardner Center for Youth and their Communities on projects related to community schools, health and wellness, and community engagement. She is passionate about community-based research as well as cross-sector and interdisciplinary collaboration to solve health and social issues. Sophia recently graduated from Stanford University with a BA in Human Biology, concentrating in Community Health and Social Justice. Her dream is to become a primary care provider working with underserved communities to promote individual and public health.

For more information about Sophia’s fellowship year, bookmark her blog: Reflections of a Stanford Shinnyo Fellow



Cody Young, Public Ally

Cody Young is the latest addition to the Gardner Center’s East Palo Alto Youth Arts & Music Center team. Her role is to organize, empower, and support a group of youth participants in the creation of the East Palo Alto Youth Art and Music Center. Prior to her Public Allies placement with the Gardner Center, Cody was the Community Project Director for The Social Awareness Network for Activism through Art (SANAA) at UCLA. At SANAA, Cody established and maintained a safe space for South Los Angeles High School students to engage in the process of critical dialogue, critical thinking, and artistic freedom. Cody has also conducted research on how Black male college students express their thoughts about their presence within higher education through music. She is passionate about empowering and valuing the voices of under-represented people in all spaces. She also brings experience in constructing yearly and quarterly funding proposals and grants, working with incarcerated youth, and community engagement. Cody holds two BAs in Anthropology and African American Studies from The University of California, Los Angeles.
