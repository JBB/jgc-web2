A Study of the Sequoia Healthcare District’s Healthy Schools Initiative


Partner Organizations

  • Sequoia Health Care District
  • Belmont-Redwood Shores School District
  • Redwood City School District
  • San Carlos School District
  • Sequoia Union High School District

Publications

  • Healthy Schools Initiative: Implementation Study in Four San Mateo County School Districts (Report & Executive Summary)

In a constrained fiscal climate, many California schools have cut or reduced funding for health and wellness programming for students. In August 2010, the Sequoia Healthcare District (SHD) launched the Healthy Schools Initiative to support four local school districts with funding for staff and programs aimed at improving the physical and emotional health of students and families.

Reaching approximately 28,000 students per year, the Healthy Schools Initiative is based on the Centers for Disease Control’s Coordinated School Health (CSH) model. The CSH model emphasizes coordination of eight core wellness components: health education, physical education and activity, health services, nutrition services, counseling and social services, a healthy school environment, health promotion for staff, and family and community involvement.

SHD is partnering with the Gardner Center to conduct a three-year implementation study of the Healthy Schools Initiative, examining coordination, awareness, and accessibility of these components across a sample of partner schools in the four districts. This study is a critical precursor to any future research on the ways that the initiative influences individual student outcomes.

During the first year of the study, the Gardner Center worked with SHD to develop a logic model and conducted a needs assessment, mapping health and wellness programs and activities in the four school districts. Year two data collection included nearly 100 structured interviews and focus groups with district and school staff, students, and parents. Researchers found that effective and purposeful coordination was associated with increased integration of health and wellness programs within and across schools and districts, reaching more students equitably and leveraging resources. Other key findings included:


  • Nearly 100% of study participants valued health and wellness in schools.
  • Study participants who were familiar with HSI believed that the initiative positively influenced awareness of health and wellness in their schools.
  • Where wellness coordinators became involved in health and wellness programs and activities, coordination was described as more strategic and intentional.

Year three of the study focuses on dissemination of research findings to audiences including parents, teachers, school district staff, and the community at large.
