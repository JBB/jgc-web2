This guide for middle school leaders describes four work areas central to developing a whole-school youth development approach based on one school's process. The guide describes preliminary evidence of progress, and includes hyperlinks to tools that can be adapted to support similar efforts in other settings.

Tools That Support Work Area 1: Engage Cross-Functional Expertise in Support of Youth
These activities engage diverse constituents in a collective visioning process that leads constituents to understand, value and ultimately seek the expertise of other school members.

Tools That Support Work Area 2: Situate Academic Learning and Achievement in the Context of a Youth Development Approach
These activities and resources help schools to build on their prior knowledge and existing practices to increase their understanding of learning in the context of youth development, and to develop a plan that expands on existing youth development practices.

Tools to Support Work Area 3: Integrate Local and Research Knowledge into Professional Development
This activity and resources guide the integration of local and research knowledge into the implementation of a youth development approach.
